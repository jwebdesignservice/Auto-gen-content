Most landing pages fail for the same five reasons.

I've audited a lot of websites this year, and the same handful of mistakes show up again and again. Fixing them, individually, can double conversion.

Here are the 5 things every landing page actually needs:

1. A clear value prop above the fold. One sentence, specific to your buyer. If they need to think "wait, what does this do?", you've already lost them.

2. One CTA, repeated. Not "Sign up" plus "Book a demo" plus "Contact sales." Pick one action. Repeat it after every section.

3. Real social proof. Logos, named testimonials with photos, specific numbers. "Trusted by thousands" is wallpaper. "200 founders shipped this month" sells.

4. Friction-free contact. Email visible. Calendar embedded. Form has 4 fields max. If they have to dig to talk to you, they won't bother.

5. Sub-2-second load. Every extra second costs around 7% of conversions. Optimise images, lazy-load below the fold, self-host fonts, run Lighthouse weekly.

Get these right and your landing page works.
